Carolina Lins Machado

I am a Research Assistant and PhD candidate in Computational Linguistics and Phonetics at the University of Zurich. My main research interest is in phonetics and its applications in biometrics, especifically examining individual variation in speech production and how speaker-specific behavior in the acoustic signal is explained by articulatory measures. I am also curious about speaker-specific behavior of monolingual and bilingual (also multilingual) speakers across their life-spans.

This is how I pronounce my name: [kaɾuˈlinɐ ˈlĩʃ maˈʃadu]

Kijk: a praat plugin to visualize articulatory trajectories


Kijk [kɛik] (meaning “look” in Dutch) is a plugin completely implemented in Praat to automatically process EMA data for the visualisation of kinematic trajectories.

Although limited to displaying only position data, a future iteration will include the calculation of velocity and acceleration. Nonetheless, at the current stand, Kijk is a simple and straightforward alternative for students and researchers wishing for a quick visualisation of kinematic trajectories, or as an educational tool for introducing undergraduate students to EMA data along with the acoustic signal. Kijk was conceptualised as an open tool, therefore we welcome feedback and collaborations in future versions of this plugin.

The plugin will be available here and can be used with data collected with the EMA devices NDI Wave and Carsten's AG501.

The plugin was developed under Praat version 6.2.13 and assumes that the EMA data was corrected for head movements and that it was translated to a coordinated system in relation to the occlusal plane.

Kijk will be presented at the ICPhS 2023.
